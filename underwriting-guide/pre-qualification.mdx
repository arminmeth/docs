## Overview

The pre-qualification phase is an initial screening conducted before the underwriting process. Its purpose is to confirm that the applicantâ€™s transaction volumes, transaction characteristics, and intended use case are compatible with our eligibility criteria.

During this phase:

- Data is self-reported by the applicant and may not yet be verified.
- The review focuses on high-level indicators of volume, transaction frequency, and rail usage.
- Pre-qualification is not an approval and does not involve a formal risk assessment.
- The outcome is intended solely to determine whether the applicant should advance to the underwriting stage.
- Verified 90-day transaction dataset from Pre-Qualification will feed into Preliminary Assessment phase of the Underwriting Process.

## Requirements

### On/Off-Ramping Transaction Data

As part of the pre-qualification review, applicants must submit 90 days of complete on/off-ramping transaction data, including:

- All payment rails
    - Same-Day ACH, Next-Day ACH, Wire, RTP, and any other instant or delayed channels used.
- All transaction outcomes
    - Successful, failed, reversed, and any other relevant statuses.
- Settlement timing data
    - For each transaction: initiation timestamp, settlement timestamp, and any intermediate status timestamps if available.
